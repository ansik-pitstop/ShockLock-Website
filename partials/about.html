About ShockLock

ShockLock is the strongest solution for failed gas struts. It is a heavy duty tool to help hold up hatches like hoods and trunks, providing a safe working environment for technicians and vehicle owners. Intuitively designed, ShockLock reduces vehicle damage liability, while optimizing engine workspace.

ShockLock is an invention from Ansik Inc a Start-up company which is the conception and ingenuity of three graduates from the University of Waterloo in Kitchener, Ontario, Canada. Shiva Bhardwaj is an Electrical and Computer Engineering graduate with an extensive background in automotive service as a result of working at his father’s garage, GB Autos, since he was 12 years old. Nitish Pahwa is a Mechanical Engineering graduate who has designed and built automotive products for companies like Linamar and ABC group. Yashin Shah is a Communications and Management Studies graduate with extensive sales and marketing experience and has been a SEMA (Speciality Equipment Market Association) for the past two years. Headquartered in the heart of Downtown Kitchener, Ansik Inc. is currently incubated through the University of Waterloo’s Velocity Foundry incubator.

After many years in his fathers mechanic shop, Shiva witnessed countless of injuries caused by failed gas struts. The number of bruised hands and stitches to the head from hoods and trunks falling down on drivers and technicians is what really motivated Shiva to build ShockLock. One incident in particular triggered the initiative to solve this problem. An older gentleman went into the trunk of his Mercedes B200 to grab some bags as he was leaving his vehicle at the shop for engine repairs. While grabbing his bags he slightly shook the vehicle which resulted in his weakened struts to fail causing his trunk to come crashing down like a guillotine. Luckily Shiva was there to catch the hatch before it smashed his back!! When Shiva asked the customer if he should order him two gas charged lift supports the response was, "No way I have looked at the price and $150 for the pair is not worth while". What shocked Shiva was the cost of being safe from your hatch was significantly overpriced, and that is where we set out to developing our simple yet cost effective technology to bring ShockLock to the automotive world.
